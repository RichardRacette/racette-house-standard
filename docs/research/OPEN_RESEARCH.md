## RQ-0001 — Preserving the Evolution of Understanding

### Question

Should the Racette House Standard preserve not only knowledge itself, but also the evolution of understanding that produced that knowledge?

### Background

Current RHS focuses on preserving human understanding through structured knowledge records.

However, the development of RHS has demonstrated that understanding often emerges through discussion, experimentation, revision, and evidence.

Future generations may benefit from understanding not only what became part of the standard, but how and why those ideas evolved.

### Hypothesis

A preservation methodology may become significantly more valuable if it preserves selected portions of its own design history.

Potential preservation layers include:

- Published Standard
- Edition History
- Design History
- Research Evidence

Each layer answers different questions:

- What?
- When?
- Why?
- How did we know?

### Open Questions

- Which design conversations deserve preservation?
- How should exploratory discussions be distinguished from authoritative methodology?
- Should design history become part of the permanent archive?
- How should reasoning be linked to published editions?
- Can preserving methodological evolution improve future understanding?

### Status

Exploratory

No changes to the standard are proposed at this time.

Future editions may revisit this question after additional research and practical experience.
