# RHS Roadmap

> *The Racette House is a living research laboratory for developing and validating a general methodology for preserving human understanding.*

---

# Purpose

This roadmap describes the intended evolution of the Racette House Standard (RHS).

Unlike a traditional software roadmap, this document tracks the maturity of the methodology rather than a list of software features.

The sequence is intentionally iterative.

Research findings may change priorities, introduce new work, or alter the direction of future phases.

---

# Guiding Principle

The objective is not to finish documentation.

The objective is to develop a methodology that can reliably preserve human understanding across generations.

Every milestone should improve the methodology through observation, validation, and evidence.

# Phase 2 — Methodology

Objective

Develop the governing standards that define how preserved knowledge should be created, validated, and maintained.

Primary deliverables

- Constitution
- Research Charter
- Reference Record Style Guide
- Validation Checklist
- Maturity Model
- Publication Policy
- Evidence Policy
- Canonical Knowledge Policy

Success criteria

- Standards are internally consistent.
- Canonical knowledge rules are established.
- Multiple reviewers could produce similar Reference Records using the published standards.

---

# Phase 3 — Household Knowledge Language (HKL)

Objective

Develop the representation language used to encode preserved understanding.

Research areas

- Entity taxonomy
- Relationship taxonomy
- Identifier conventions
- Confidence model
- Temporal representation
- Evidence relationships
- Preservation Debt
- Access classification
- Machine-readable structures

Success criteria

- Complex household knowledge can be represented consistently.
- Records remain understandable without their original author.
- Representation supports future AI retrieval without confusing evidence and interpretation.

---

# Phase 4 — Reference Record Validation

Objective

Test the methodology using real household knowledge.

Initial candidate records

- RR-0001 — Motawi Star Tile
- RR-0002 — The Pit
- RR-0003 — Organized Maximalism

Research goals

- Identify missing standards.
- Identify ambiguity.
- Measure documentation effort.
- Improve repeatability.
- Refine HKL.

Success criteria

Reference Records consistently preserve:

- facts
- meaning
- provenance
- uncertainty
- relationships
- evidence

---

# Phase 5 — Archive Expansion

Objective

Expand from demonstration records to a comprehensive household archive.

Potential categories

- People
- Objects
- Spaces
- Collections
- Systems
- Devices
- Traditions
- Recipes
- Projects
- Maintenance
- Household vocabulary
- Family history

Success criteria

The archive becomes useful for daily household operations while continuing to validate the methodology.

---

# Phase 6 — Longitudinal Research

Objective

Study how preserved knowledge changes over time.

Research questions

- How should revisions be governed?
- What should never change?
- How should conflicting testimony be preserved?
- How should forgotten information be represented?
- How should Preservation Debt evolve?

Success criteria

The methodology remains stable while adapting to newly discovered evidence.

---

# Phase 7 — Generalization

Objective

Determine whether RHS can extend beyond the Racette House.

Potential research environments

- Other households
- Personal archives
- Museums
- Historical societies
- Small organizations
- Community archives

Success criteria

Independent users can apply RHS without requiring direct assistance from its original author.

# How Success Is Measured

Progress is measured by improvements in the methodology rather than repository size.

Questions to ask include:

- Can another person understand the record?
- Can another person recreate the record?
- Is uncertainty preserved?
- Is evidence traceable?
- Does the methodology remain internally consistent?
- Does the archive become more understandable over time?

---

# Living Document

This roadmap is intentionally non-binding.

Research may invalidate assumptions, introduce new phases, or reorder priorities.

Changes should be documented rather than hidden.

The roadmap itself should evolve alongside the methodology.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The Racette House Archive is the first implementation.

The larger ambition is to better understand how people preserve meaning—not merely information—and to develop practical standards that help future generations inherit understanding rather than fragments of memory.

The methodology should remain evidence-driven, transparent, and adaptable while preserving attribution and respecting the integrity of the historical record.
