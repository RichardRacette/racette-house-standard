# Contributing to the Racette House Standard

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the Racette House Standard (RHS).

RHS exists to preserve human understanding.

Every contribution should strengthen that mission by improving the methodology while maintaining clarity, consistency, long-term reliability, and historical integrity.

This document explains how the standard evolves and how contributions are evaluated during the active research phase of the project.

---

# Project Stewardship

The Racette House Standard is currently stewarded by its original author.

At this stage of development, RHS follows a **steward-led governance model**.

Community participation is encouraged through discussion, questions, issue reports, examples, research, and proposed improvements.

The project steward is responsible for determining when and how accepted ideas become part of an official edition of the standard.

This approach allows the methodology to mature intentionally while remaining open to new ideas and evidence.

As the project evolves, its governance model may also evolve. Any future transition to a broader governance structure will be documented through published editions of the standard.

---

# Guiding Principle

Contributions should improve the Racette House Standard—not simply change it.

Novelty alone is not a sufficient reason to modify the methodology.

Preference should always be given to improvements that increase:

- clarity
- consistency
- interoperability
- long-term maintainability
- preservation quality
- practical usability

Whenever possible, changes should reduce complexity rather than increase it.

---

# Types of Contributions

Examples of valuable contributions include:

## Methodology

- improvements to preservation methodology
- classification systems
- validation approaches
- lifecycle management

## Household Knowledge Language (HKL)

- language proposals
- structural improvements
- semantic clarification
- compatibility enhancements

## Documentation

- clarification
- improved examples
- editorial corrections
- reference material

## Research

- preservation case studies
- archival observations
- validation results
- comparative analysis
- lessons learned from real-world implementations

## Reference Implementations

Examples demonstrating how the methodology performs in practice are especially valuable.

---

# Evidence-Based Development

Methodological improvements should be supported whenever practical by:

- documented experience
- reference implementations
- reproducible examples
- research
- reasoned analysis

Well-supported proposals are generally more valuable than unsupported opinions.

Not every contribution requires formal research, but meaningful changes should be accompanied by an explanation of why they improve the standard.

---

# Stability Before Novelty

RHS values stability over unnecessary change.

Changes should be introduced deliberately and only when they clearly improve the methodology.

Maintaining continuity across published editions is considered an important design objective.

Breaking changes should be avoided unless they provide substantial long-term benefit.

---

# Experimental Ideas

Innovation is encouraged.

Not every idea, however, should immediately become part of the standard.

New concepts may progress through stages such as:

Research

↓

Experimental

↓

Validated

↓

Published Standard

This progression helps distinguish promising ideas from established methodology.

---

# Publication Authority

Discussion, issues, pull requests, and accepted changes contribute to the development of RHS.

However, the official Racette House Standard is defined only by its published editions.

Git history records the evolution of the project.

Published editions define the methodology.

---

# Attribution

RHS seeks to encourage collaboration while preserving appropriate attribution.

Contributors should receive recognition for meaningful ideas and improvements.

Attribution strengthens the historical record of the project while maintaining the coherence of the standard.

---

# Decision Transparency

Significant methodological decisions should be documented.

Whenever practical, accepted changes should explain:

- what changed
- why the change was made
- alternatives considered
- expected impact
- edition in which the change became authoritative

Understanding why decisions were made is itself an important part of preserving knowledge.

---

# Historical Integrity

The history of the methodology should remain understandable.

Published editions should improve the standard without obscuring earlier reasoning.

Past ideas, including those later superseded, remain valuable for understanding how the methodology evolved.

---

# Code of Conduct

Participation in this project is expected to follow the project's Code of Conduct.

Respectful discussion, constructive disagreement, intellectual honesty, and evidence-based reasoning are expected from all participants.

---

# Thank You

Every thoughtful contribution helps improve the long-term preservation of human understanding.

Whether through research, discussion, examples, corrections, or new ideas, your participation contributes to the continuing evolution of the Racette House Standard.
