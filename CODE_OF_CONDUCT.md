# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

The Racette House Standard (RHS) exists to advance the preservation of human understanding.

This Code of Conduct establishes the expectations for everyone participating in the project, whether through discussion, research, documentation, issue reporting, or proposed improvements.

Our goal is to cultivate a community that values curiosity, respect, evidence, and thoughtful collaboration.

---

# Our Principles

Participants are expected to contribute in ways that strengthen both the project and the people involved.

We strive to build a community characterized by:

- intellectual curiosity
- respectful communication
- thoughtful disagreement
- evidence-based reasoning
- historical integrity
- long-term thinking
- generosity in sharing knowledge

---

# Expected Behavior

We encourage participants to:

- Ask questions with genuine curiosity.
- Share knowledge openly and accurately.
- Support ideas with reasoning or evidence whenever practical.
- Accept correction gracefully.
- Offer constructive feedback rather than personal criticism.
- Recognize uncertainty where it exists.
- Preserve context when discussing previous work.
- Help improve the clarity and accessibility of the methodology.
- Respect the time and effort of other contributors.
- Give appropriate credit for meaningful contributions.

---

# Constructive Disagreement

Disagreement is both expected and valuable.

Ideas should be challenged.

People should not.

Healthy discussion focuses on improving the methodology rather than winning arguments.

Changing one's mind in response to better evidence is viewed as a strength rather than a weakness.

---

# Research Ethics

RHS values careful reasoning over certainty.

Participants should:

- distinguish observations from conclusions
- identify assumptions when appropriate
- acknowledge uncertainty
- avoid presenting speculation as established fact
- remain open to new evidence

The goal is not to defend existing ideas but to continually improve the quality of the methodology.

---

# Respect for Contributors

Everyone deserves to be treated with professionalism and respect.

Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, personal attacks, or deliberately disruptive behavior have no place within this project.

This includes behavior based on, but not limited to:

- identity
- background
- nationality
- disability
- religion
- sexual orientation
- gender
- age
- experience level

Professional disagreement is welcome.

Personal hostility is not.

---

# Stewardship

During the active research phase, the Racette House Standard follows a steward-led governance model.

The project steward is responsible for maintaining the long-term coherence of the methodology while carefully considering community feedback and research contributions.

This responsibility carries an obligation to make decisions thoughtfully, transparently, and in service of the project's mission.

---

# Resolving Concerns

If behavior is inconsistent with this Code of Conduct, participants are encouraged to respectfully raise the concern with the project steward.

Concerns should be evaluated fairly, impartially, and with the goal of preserving a constructive environment for everyone involved.

Responses should prioritize education and resolution whenever practical.

Persistent or serious violations may result in removal from project participation.

---

# A Living Culture

This Code of Conduct is itself part of the Racette House Standard.

As the project matures, the community may refine these expectations through the same evidence-based publication process used for the rest of the methodology.

---

# Closing

The Racette House Standard exists because human understanding is worth preserving.

Every participant helps shape not only the methodology, but the culture surrounding it.

We hope this project becomes known not only for the quality of its ideas, but for the quality of the conversations that produced them.
