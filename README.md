# Racette House Standard (RHS)

> *The Racette House is a living research laboratory for developing and validating a general methodology for preserving human understanding.*

---

## What is RHS?

The Racette House Standard (RHS) is an ongoing research project focused on a deceptively simple question:

**How can human understanding be preserved so that it remains meaningful decades into the future?**

Most documentation preserves information.

RHS seeks to preserve understanding.

The project explores how memories, objects, spaces, traditions, systems, relationships, and lived experiences can be documented with enough context that a future reader—or an AI system—can understand not only *what* existed, but *why it mattered*.

---

## Project Architecture

The work is organized into three complementary layers.

### RHS — Racette House Standard

The methodology.

RHS defines the principles, governance, editorial standards, validation rules, and publication process used to preserve knowledge.

---

### HKL — Household Knowledge Language

The representation.

HKL defines how preserved knowledge is structured, connected, referenced, and interpreted.

---

### Racette House Archive

The reference implementation.

The Archive applies RHS and HKL to a real household, creating a living body of Reference Records, evidence, and operational knowledge that continuously tests and improves the methodology.

---

## Research Philosophy

This project treats the Racette House as a living laboratory.

Every Reference Record, experiment, and methodological revision contributes to a larger goal:

Developing practical standards for preserving human understanding in a way that remains useful across generations.

The methodology is expected to evolve as new evidence is gathered and new questions emerge.

---

## Guiding Principles

The project is built around several core principles.

- Preserve understanding rather than information.
- Distinguish evidence from interpretation.
- Record uncertainty rather than hiding it.
- Make important claims traceable.
- Prefer governed revision over silent correction.
- Design for both human readers and future AI systems.
- Treat preservation as an ongoing process rather than a completed task.

---

## Long-Term Vision

The long-term objective is to develop a practical, evidence-based methodology that helps people preserve not only what happened, but what it meant.

While the Racette House serves as the first research environment, the ideas developed here are intended to inform broader conversations about knowledge preservation, personal archives, digital memory, and AI-assisted understanding.
